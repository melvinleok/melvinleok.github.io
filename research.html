Core Research

Geometric Foundations of Digital Twins

Bridging the gap between physical reality and virtual simulation through a unified geometric framework for hierarchical, interconnected systems.

The Physical-Digital Loop

Digital twins are more than high-fidelity virtual representations; they are tightly coupled with their physical counterparts in a continuous feedback loop. The physical system informs the digital twin via sensors and data assimilation, while the digital twin informs the physical counterpart via decision and control.

Our research develops a unified geometric framework for constructing hierarchical digital twins. This approach couples continuous high-fidelity models with reduced-order surrogates and data-aware machine learning, ensuring consistency across all levels of parameterization.

Variational Integrators

Structure-preserving numerical methods that preserve momentum and symplectic structure. These methods yield superior qualitative accuracy and energy behavior over long timescales.

Dirac Mechanics

A geometric description of interconnected, multi-physics systems. Dirac structures encode holonomic and nonholonomic constraints essential for modeling multibody dynamics and circuits.

Manifold Autonomy

Configuration spaces for robots and autonomous vehicles naturally reside on Lie groups. Our work ensures robust, accurate, and geometric structure-preserving discretizations on these manifolds.

Symplectic Optimization

We systematically discretize the flow of Bregman Hamiltonians to obtain time-adaptive optimization algorithms that outperform standard accelerated gradient methods.

Physics-Informed Learning

Training deep neural networks can be viewed through the lens of adjoint sensitivity analysis. We demonstrate that the preservation of presymplectic structures is essential for efficient training of physics-informed neural networks and interconnected systems.
