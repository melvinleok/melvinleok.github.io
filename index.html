The Challenge

Closing the Foundational Gaps in Digital Twins

Digital twins require a rigorous mathematical foundation where the physical and digital counterparts are tightly coupled in a feedback loop. We leverage computational geometric mechanics to construct integrators using discrete analogues of variational mechanics.

Variational Integrators

Structure-preserving numerical methods, based on a discrete Hamilton’s principle, that exhibit excellent energy behavior and preserve momentum and symplectic structure over long timescales.

Manifold Awareness

Nonlinear manifolds, such as Lie groups and homogeneous spaces, arise naturally as configuration manifolds for robots and autonomous drones.

Accelerated Optimization

Symplectic optimization discretizes Bregman Hamiltonians to achieve convergence rates of O(1/tᵖ), outperforming standard Nesterov methods.

Mathematically-Informed Learning

The preservation of presymplectic structures is essential for efficient adjoint sensitivity computation when training physics-informed neural networks.
