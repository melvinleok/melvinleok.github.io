Dirac Mechanics & Port-Hamiltonian Systems

Interconnected dynamical systems abstract the interactions between subsystems in terms of constraints. Dirac mechanics and geometry provide a natural setting for describing these degenerate, interconnected, holonomic, and nonholonomic systems.

"Dirac structures can encode the holonomic and nonholonomic constraints that model the interconnection. This is used in the port-Hamiltonian approach to obtain implicit Hamilton's equations that are differential-algebraic equations for the interconnected system."

Port-Hamiltonian systems provide a geometric description of hierarchical, interconnected, multiphysics systems based on Dirac geometry. This foundation allows us to construct hierarchical digital twins for simulation, optimization, and control.
